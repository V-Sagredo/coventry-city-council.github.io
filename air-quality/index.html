WHO Global Ambient Air Quality Database (update 2018)

According to the World Health Organisation (WHO), as urban air quality declines, the risk of stroke, heart disease, lung cancer, and chronic and acute respiratory diseases, including asthma, increases for the people who live in them. The WHO air quality guideline is PM10 of less than 20μg/m3 in one year ; and PM2.5 of less than 10μg/m3 in one year. Find out more at: http://www.who.int/airpollution/data/cities/en/.<
